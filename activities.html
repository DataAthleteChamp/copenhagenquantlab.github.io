Our Activities

Copenhagen Quant Lab runs five core programs designed to prepare members for careers in quantitative trading and research.

Weekly Meetups

When: Every Wednesday, 18:00-20:00
Where: DTU Building 101, Room 3C

Structured sessions combining short lectures, problem-solving, and discussion. Each session includes a take-home problem set reviewed the following week.

Sample Topics:

  • Expected Value & Probability Foundations: Coin flip games, Monty Hall problem, birthday paradox, conditional probability
  • Options Pricing Fundamentals: Black-Scholes intuition, put-call parity, Greeks, volatility concepts
  • Market Making Basics: Order book dynamics, inventory management, adverse selection, bid-ask spreads
  • Algorithmic Trading: Momentum strategies, mean reversion, backtesting considerations, risk management
  • Interview Practice: Probability brainteasers, mental math drills, trade structuring, case studies

Problem Sets

Weekly problem sets progress from foundational mathematics through interview-level complexity, building the technical skills essential for quant finance roles.

Progression:

  • Early Semester: Probability distributions, expected value calculations, combinatorics, basic options
  • Mid-Semester: Conditional probability, Bayesian thinking, stochastic processes, derivatives pricing
  • Late Semester: Game theory scenarios, optimal strategy problems, market microstructure, full case studies

All problems reviewed collaboratively the following week, with multiple solution approaches discussed. Archive of 50+ problems with full solutions available to members.

Mock Interviews

Format: Monthly 30-minute one-on-one sessions
Focus: Simulating real quantitative trading and research interviews

Mock interviews cover:

  • Probability puzzles and brainteasers
  • Mental math and quick calculations
  • Trade scenarios and market intuition
  • Behavioral questions and culture fit

Feedback: Detailed written feedback provided within 48 hours covering technical performance, communication style, and improvement areas.

Interviewers: Experienced board members, alumni with internship experience, and industry contacts as our network grows.

Poker & Chess

Poker and chess aren't just social events‚Äîthey're practical training for skills directly applicable to trading.

Poker Night (Weekly - Fridays 19:00)

What you learn:

  • Probability assessment: Pot odds, implied odds, hand equity calculations
  • Risk management: Position sizing, bankroll management, variance handling
  • Reading incomplete information: Opponent tendencies, bet patterns, Bayesian updates
  • Emotional control: Handling variance, avoiding tilt, disciplined decision-making

Chess Tournament (Monthly)

What you learn:

  • Pattern recognition: Identifying tactical motifs and strategic themes
  • Calculating variations: Thinking several moves ahead, evaluating positions
  • Strategic planning: Long-term goals vs. short-term tactics
  • Time management: Making quality decisions under pressure

Many successful traders credit poker and chess as formative experiences that developed their analytical thinking and risk management skills.
