Learning Resources

Curated materials for mastering quantitative finance, from probability foundations through advanced trading strategies.

ðŸ“Š Probability & Statistics

Foundational knowledge for all quantitative work.

Fifty Challenging Problems in Probability with Solutions by Frederick Mosteller
Classic collection of elegant probability puzzles. Perfect for building intuition and problem-solving skills. Start here if you're comfortable with basic probability.
Introduction to Probability by Joseph Blitzstein & Jessica Hwang
Comprehensive textbook covering probability theory from fundamentals through advanced topics. Excellent for systematic learning. Harvard's Stat 110 course uses this textâ€”videos available free online.
The Drunkard's Walk by Leonard Mlodinow
Accessible introduction to probability and randomness for building intuition. Great for understanding why probability matters in real-world decisions.
Brilliant.org Probability Course
Interactive online learning with immediate feedback. Covers expected value, distributions, conditional probability, and more. Excellent for self-paced learning.
MIT OpenCourseWare 18.05: Introduction to Probability and Statistics
Free lecture notes, assignments, and solutions. Rigorous treatment suitable for technical students. Includes R programming for statistical analysis.

ðŸ“ˆ Options & Derivatives

Understanding financial instruments and pricing theory.

Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives by John Hull
Industry standard textbook. Focus on chapters 1-15 (options fundamentals, Black-Scholes, Greeks, binomial trees) and 20-23 (advanced topics). Dense but comprehensive.
Paul Wilmott Introduces Quantitative Finance by Paul Wilmott
More intuitive than Hull, with practical examples and trading perspectives. Excellent for understanding "why" behind the mathematics.
Coursera: Financial Engineering and Risk Management by Columbia University
Video lectures on derivatives pricing, portfolio optimization, and risk management. Includes Python programming assignments.
OptionsPlaybook.com
Interactive tool for visualizing options strategies (spreads, straddles, butterflies). Essential for building intuition about payoff profiles.
Project: Build Black-Scholes Calculator
Implement Black-Scholes formula in Python, calculate Greeks, visualize how option prices change with parameters. Excellent hands-on learning.

ðŸ’» Algorithmic Trading

Systematic strategy development and implementation.

Algorithmic Trading: Winning Strategies and Their Rationale by Ernest Chan
Practical guide to developing trading strategies. Covers mean reversion, momentum, statistical arbitrage with Python examples.
Advances in Financial Machine Learning by Marcos LÃ³pez de Prado
Advanced techniques for applying machine learning to trading. Covers backtesting, feature engineering, risk management. For experienced coders.
QuantConnect Platform
Cloud-based backtesting platform with free tier. Code strategies in Python, backtest on historical data, paper trade live. Active community forum.
Backtrader (Python Library)
Open-source backtesting framework. More control than QuantConnect, steeper learning curve. Excellent for serious strategy development.
Yahoo Finance & yfinance Library
Free historical stock data for backtesting. Simple Python library: pip install yfinance and you're ready to download data.
Starter Project: Moving Average Crossover
Build simple momentum strategy: buy when short MA crosses above long MA, sell on reverse. Analyze returns, drawdowns, Sharpe ratio.
