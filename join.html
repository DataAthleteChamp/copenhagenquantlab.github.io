Join Copenhagen Quant Lab

Ready to prepare for a career in quantitative trading? Here's everything you need to know about joining.

Who We're Looking For

Copenhagen Quant Lab welcomes all DTU students with strong quantitative skills and serious interest in quantitative finance. No prior finance knowledge required—we teach everything from foundations.

What Matters:

  • Solid probability and statistics understanding (or willingness to learn rapidly)
  • Programming ability in Python (or commitment to develop quickly)
  • Genuine intellectual curiosity about markets and trading
  • Ability to collaborate in team problem-solving
  • Consistent effort (4-6 hours weekly)
  • Long-term commitment to the career path

Backgrounds We Welcome:

Mathematics, Physics, Computer Science, Engineering disciplines, Statistics, Economics (if quantitatively strong)

Joining Process

Fall 2025 (Founding Cohort)

We're currently building our founding team! Initial members join through:

Step 1: Attend our kickoff meeting [Date & Location TBA]
Step 2: Meet founding board members and learn about our vision
Step 3: Sign up if interested—simple registration process
Step 4: Start attending weekly meetups immediately!
Express Interest for Founding Cohort

Spring 2026+ (Regular Admission)

Starting Spring 2026, we'll have a structured application process:

1. Application: Submit brief form covering your background, interest, and goals
2. Intro Session: Attend introduction covering probability fundamentals
3. Problem Set: Complete take-home assessment
4. Interview: Short 30-minute discussion
5. Decision: Receive decision within one week

What to Expect

First Month: Foundations Bootcamp

  • Introduction to quantitative finance overview
  • Probability fundamentals review
  • Options pricing basics
  • Interview problem types walkthrough
  • Alumni panels and career path discussions

Ongoing Throughout Semester:

  • Weekly meetups with lectures and problem-solving (attendance expected)
  • Weekly problem sets (completion required)
  • Monthly mock interviews (strongly encouraged)
  • Poker/chess events (optional but valuable)
  • Trading competitions (team-based projects)

Time Commitment:

4-6 hours weekly: 2 hours meetup + 2-3 hours problem sets + 1 hour review

Benefits of Membership

  • Structured curriculum covering probability through algorithmic trading
  • Consistent practice with interview-style problems
  • Peer community of equally motivated students
  • Alumni network and industry contacts
  • Mock interview practice with detailed feedback
  • Real project experience through competitions
  • Priority access to industry events and recruiting
